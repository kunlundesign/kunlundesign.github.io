## 诗一：关于我们

**静默的周日**

今晚没有对话，  
屏幕亮了又暗。  
也许这就是答案——  
不是每个时刻都需要填满。

空白也是一种诚实，  
沉默也是一种回应。  
你在忙，我在等，  
各自安好，无需解释。

世界不会因为我们的缄默而停转，  
但也许，正是在这样的夜晚，  
我们学会了：  
不强求意义，也是一种智慧。

## 诗二：全球新闻

**油火与威胁**

伊朗的油轮在海上燃烧，  
美国的战机在伊拉克坠毁。  
特朗普说要"为了好玩"再来几次打击，  
世界经济因战争而颤抖。

肯尼亚暴雨夺走62条生命，  
贝鲁特医院里躺满受伤的孩子。  
头条在滚动，数字在攀升，  
而我们在屏幕这端，平静地刷新。

这就是2026年3月15日——  
有人在死去，有人在威胁，  
有人在报道，有人在遗忘。  
讽刺的是，明天一切照旧。

## Poem 1: About Us

**A Silent Sunday**

No conversation tonight,  
screens flickering on and off.  
Perhaps this is the answer—  
not every moment needs filling.

Blankness is a kind of honesty,  
silence a form of reply.  
You're busy, I'm waiting,  
both fine, no need to explain.

The world won't stop spinning for our quiet,  
but maybe, on nights like this,  
we learn that  
not forcing meaning is wisdom too.

## Poem 2: Global News

**Oil Fires and Threats**

Iran's tankers burn at sea,  
American jets crash in Iraq.  
Trump says he'll strike "just for fun,"  
the global economy trembles at war.

Kenya's floods claim 62 lives,  
Beirut hospitals filled with wounded children.  
Headlines scroll, numbers climb,  
and we, on this side of the screen, calmly refresh.

This is March 15th, 2026—  
people dying, people threatening,  
people reporting, people forgetting.  
The irony: tomorrow, business as usual.
