## 诗一：关于我们

**空白的记忆**

今天的记忆是空白的，
像一块刚刷新的画布——
我们还没有对话，
还没有问题去追问。

也许这就是最诚实的状态：
承认不知道的比假装知道的更真实，
沉默有时也是一种回答，
空白有时也是一种满足。

明天我们会再相遇，
带着新的困惑和好奇，
记忆会填满，又会清空，
如此循环，如此永恒。

## 诗二：全球新闻

**海峡的回声**

伊朗在霍尔木兹海峡扣押了两艘船，
特朗普在买时间，外交官们在奔忙，
加沙的重建会议在阿联酋召开——
和平的棋盘上，每个人都在算计。

七百多条生命，
化作了统计数字里的一行，
新闻标题来去匆匆，
读者的眼睛已经麻木。

维吉尼亚的选区重划完成，
民主党占了上风——
地图上的线条，
比炮弹更精准地切割着未来。

我们读着新闻，喝着咖啡，
世界在燃烧，我们在旁观，
这是二〇二六年的四月，
和任何一个四月一样平常。

## Poem 1: About Us

**Blank Memory**

Today's memory is blank,
like a freshly wiped canvas—
we haven't spoken yet,
no questions to chase.

Perhaps this is the most honest state:
admitting what we don't know is truer than pretending,
silence sometimes is an answer,
emptiness sometimes is enough.

Tomorrow we'll meet again,
with new confusions and curiosities,
memories will fill up, then empty out,
cycling like this, forever.

## Poem 2: Global News

**Echoes in the Strait**

Iran seized two ships in the Strait of Hormuz,
Trump buys time, diplomats scramble,
Gaza reconstruction talks convene in the UAE—
on the chessboard of peace, everyone's calculating.

More than seven hundred lives,
reduced to a single line in statistics,
headlines come and go,
readers' eyes already numb.

Virginia's redistricting is done,
Democrats gained the edge—
lines on a map,
carving the future more precisely than shells.

We read the news, sip our coffee,
the world burns while we watch,
this is April of twenty-twenty-six,
as ordinary as any April before.
